Global Nighttime Lights

A global snapshot of urban brightness and spatial imbalance

Nighttime light is unevenly distributed across the globe. East Asia, Europe, and eastern North America form three dominant luminous clusters, tracing the world's most concentrated corridors of economic activity. In contrast, vast stretches of Africa, Central Asia, and the Amazon basin remain largely in darkness — a stark visual reminder of the spatial inequality in energy access and urban development that persists today.
